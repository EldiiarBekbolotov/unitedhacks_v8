About Hack United

A nonprofit that treats soft skills as part of the craft.

Hack United hosts United Hacks. Across seven prior events, more than 5,000 people from over 50 countries have shown up to build. We care about teamwork, communication, and presentation as much as the code.

Rules

Original work, built this weekend

  • Ages 13 and up. Students and professionals both welcome.
  • Projects must be original and built during the hackathon.
  • Teams of up to 4.
  • Submit a demo video, GitHub repo, and project description.
  • Follow the code of conduct. Keep the room respectful.

Common questions

What is a hackathon?

A weekend where people learn, build projects, and meet each other. Coding matters, but so does collaboration.

What should my project be about?

Whatever you want: web, mobile, hardware, or something else. Tracks and themes get announced at opening ceremony.

What if I don't know how to code?

Plenty of beginners show up. Workshops, mentors, and docs are there so you can start from zero.

Is it remote?

Yes. United Hacks V8 is fully remote. Join from anywhere with internet.

How does judging work?

Industry judges (including folks from places like Oracle, Amazon, and Walmart) score creativity, practicality, presentation, and technical complexity.

Is it free?

Yes. Entry, workshops, and resources are free worldwide.

How will we communicate during the event?

Discord carries announcements, mentorship, and most of the conversation.

Who can participate?

Anyone 13 or older, anywhere in the world. No prior experience required.

How many people are on a team?

One to four. If you need teammates, find them in Discord.

What timezone is the event in?

Eastern Standard Time. The schedule is built for a global crowd across 50+ countries.
